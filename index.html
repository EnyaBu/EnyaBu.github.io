My Research

My research interests are the following: Human-Computer Interaction, Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, Natural Language Processing, Deep Learning, Neural Networks, Time Series Analysis.

To chase this path, I worked in the Socio-Technical Interaction Research Lab under Dr. Pamela Wisniewski studying HCI and how teens interact on social media (and how we can keep them safe). I worked at Robotics and Autonomous Systems Laboratory under Dr. Nilanjan Sarkar where I continued my HCI research and where I also dabbled in Machine Learning.

I led research sessions and participant interviews for our HCI research project, which evaluated the usability and effectiveness of safety tools and nudges for minors on social media platforms. I also recruited 20+ teen participants for our research study by contacting schools and using social media, and ssisted in preparing and modifying IRB protocols to ensure ethical research practices involving minors. Here I am, at the Vanderbilt Undergraduate Research Fair!

I presented my paper "Enhancing athletic performance through AI: An iterative prompt engineering approach for LLM-based coaching feedback" at HCI International 2025! I had the wonderful opportunity to work as a student volunteer as well, and had a great experience in Sweden. In addition to prompt engineering an LLM coach, I also studied neural networks, specifically siamese neural networks to detect the differences between a professional's movements compared to a novice's.
